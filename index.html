About

Hello and welcome to my website! I am a PhD student at the Leiden Observatory, NL under the supervision of Prof. Ewine van Dishoeck. I am originally from a small town in Iran but moved to St Andrews, UK for my undergraduate studies in astrophysics. Then I did my MPhil in astrophysics at Cambridge, UK under the supervision of Prof. Cathie Clarke before moving to Leiden for my PhD.

Research

I am interested in star and planet formation. My PhD mainly focuses on chemistry in the embedded phase of stars (Class 0/I).

Observations

My observational studies have mainly focused on using ALMA to observe the spectra of protostars. These spectra can be used to further understand the chemistry of planets that start forming at early stages of star formation (see below for publications). One of my projects focused on observations of two Class 0 sources (B1-c and S68N). I modelled their spectra using CASSIS for the observed nitrogen-bearing complex organic molecules.

Modelling

I have a wide range of modelling experiences. I have used FARGO3D to assess planet migration in protoplanetary discs, CLOUDY to look at the elemental chemistry of accretion columns in T Tauri discs and RADMC3D to study complex organics around protostars (see below for publications). Here I show the results from dusty hydrodynamical simulations of FARGO that I then made their simulated observations using CASA.
